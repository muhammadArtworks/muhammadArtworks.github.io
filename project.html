What is ATS and How Does It Work?

ATS stands for Applicant Tracking System — a software used by employers to manage the hiring process. Over 90% of companies, including Fortune 500s, rely on ATS to filter and sort incoming resumes before a human ever sees them.

How ATS Works

Job Application Submission When you apply for a job, your resume is uploaded into the employer’s Applicant Tracking System (ATS), which scans and reads your resume by breaking it down into key sections such as education, work experience, and skills. The ATS then compares your content to the job description, looking for specific keywords and phrases. If your resume lacks the right formatting or relevant keywords, it may be rejected automatically. Finally, the system assigns a score or ranking to your resume based on how well it matches the job requirements, and only the highest-ranking resumes are forwarded to recruiters or hiring managers for review.
